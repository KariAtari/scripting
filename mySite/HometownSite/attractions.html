Medicine Hat holds a lot of Indigenous history which is a big draw in for tourists. The name of the city comes from the native word “saamis” which translates to medicine man’s hat. Specifically, the legend was about a member of the Blackfoot nation. However, Medicine Hat is on the traditional lands of the Kainai (Blood), Piikani (Peigan), Stoney Nakoda, and Tsuut'ina (Sarcee) as well as the Cree, Sioux, and the Saulteaux bands of the Ojibwa peoples. At the World’s Tallest Teepee, tourists can learn more about the history and culture of the indigenous people of Medicine Hat with local guides.

There is also Medalta Potteries, a historic clay museum that tells the story of Medicine Hat’s industrial developments with clay and natural gas. Just as there are opportunities to learn, there are places like the Esplanade to watch performances and shows, as well as see themed exhibits that rotate all year. For places to hang out with friends and family, there are parks such as Echo Dale and Police Point to enjoy
