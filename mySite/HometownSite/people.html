Medicine Hat is made up of many distinct cultural and ethnic groups. Many of which Indigenous peoples like the Blackfoot Confederacy, Nakoda/Assiniboine, Cree, and Metis. According to the Government of Alberta Website, “In 2021, 8.54% of the general population of Medicine Hat identified as a visible minority.” With this, there are many religions being practiced in the city. According to Statistics Canada in 2021, the most common religion is Christianity.

The city strives to recognize and welcome everyone, including recognizing sexual orientation and gender identity. A good portion of the population is made up of older generations. Note that their views may be considered very traditional, but that is not the case for the rest of the city. There are many Pride events and clubs in schools for LBGTQ+ groups to have a safe place. Generally, most residents in the city try to help others whenever they can.
